#Machine Learning and AI
Research on the use of machine learning and AI to advance the measurement, monitoring, and mapping of biodiversity and conservation efforts. This includes the development of new foundation models across modalities (such as text, remote sensing, and images) with conservation, biogeography, and macroecological applications, as well as novel benchmarks that measure AI capabilities in this field. Additionally, we welcome thought leadership and perspectives on the promises and perils of integrating AI into conservation practices.

#Biodiversity Monitoring
Research on in situ biodiversity monitoring utilizing technologies such as acoustics, environmental DNA (eDNA), and camera traps. There is a specific focus on multi-modal approaches used simultaneously to quantify integrated biodiversity patterns at large spatial scales. Particular focus on comparing the effectiveness of different techniques and technologies, including sensitivity to biases and uncertainty. This section also includes papers designing and analysising the behaviour of biodiversity metrics.

#Remote Sensing of Biodiversity
Research into the application of remote sensing for the quantification and classification of biodiversity and ecosystems at both local and global scales. This encompasses all sensor technologies—including drones, airborne and satellite LiDAR, radar, and optical sensors (particularly hyperspectral). Topics should cover the development of new sensor technologies, the creation of novel algorithms for ecosystem monitoring, advancements in leveraging existing sensors to derive critical biodiversity information, and in underlying frameworks that define biodiveristy and ecosystem

#Conservation Science
Thought leadership and perspectives on the current state of conservation science, including evaluations of what is and is not working in global implementation and policy. This section welcomes reflections on the directions taken by conservation actors (governments, NGOs, and private entities), analyses of past successes and failures, and strategic visions for the future of the field.

#Biodiversity pricing
Research discussing the pricing of biodiversity, ecosystems, and nature in traditional financial markets, including dependencies on ecosystem services. We welcome disucssion on how biodiversity is measured in such studies, what effects biodiversity impact and risk exposure has on asset prices, case studies for how nature-related risks affect companies, and new datasets developed by financial economists to study these phenomena. This section also includes papers on bheavioural changes within companies to reduce their impact on nature and the policies and pressures that induce these changes.

#Ecological Condition
Papers discussing the concept of ecological condition and its quantification. This includes the introduction of new datasets, critical analyses of existing data, and applications of these datasets to novel questions at global or local scales (provided local datasets are used to generate new, scalable frameworks or approaches). We also welcome discussions on how ecological condition is operationalized and how it feeds into global agreements, such as the Global Biodiversity Framework, or efforts to quantify and commoditize biodiversity.

#Global Biodiversity Data
Papers that analyze global biodiversity datasets to generate new insights into macro-ecological patterns and processes. This section also includes research detailing the development of new global biodiversity datasets or novel biodiversity indicators.

#Ecological model verification and validation
Papers that analyse the external validity of ecological models and biodiversity maps by comparing model predictions to on-ground data collection. This section also includes research analysing the sensitivity of global models to data biases and modelling decisions. 

#Corporate biodiversity accounting tools
Papers designing or analysing ways to measure corporate impact or dependency on nature, biodiversity, and ecosystems. This section also includes developments in the life-cycle literature that model human impact on nature and developments of global or regional standards for biodiversity accounting.

#Anthropogenic impacts on nature
Papers identifying and analysing ways in which anthropogenic impacts change the state-of-nature. Impacts include, but are not limited to, climate change, pollution, land and seascape change, direct exploitation, and invasive alien species. This section includes both novel field studies and larger global analyses, reviews, and meta-analyses.
